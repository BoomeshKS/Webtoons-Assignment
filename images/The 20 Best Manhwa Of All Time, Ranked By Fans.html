Though manga is popular all over the world, Japan isn't the only country that puts out amazing comics - there are also some outstanding series coming out of Korea. For those who haven't yet dipped their toes into manhwa (Korean comics), there are tons of fabulous series that have been translated into English to choose from. In fact, we've got a list of 20 of the top manhwa of all time.

While some manhwa have been collected into volumes, many others are available to read on an online platform called Webtoon. If you're an anime fan, you might know a few of them: the recent hit Solo Leveling and the isekai romance Why Raeliana Ended Up at the Duke's Mansion have been made into anime. These are excellent manwha, but there's more where that came from. If you're a Raeliana fan, you might also like Who Made Me A Princess, which features a similar plot. Love Solo Leveling? Try out another action series like The Breaker. Looking for something romantic that isn't an isekai? Cheese in the Trap and Seasons of Bloom offer realistic and nuanced romantic tales.

Below are some of the greatest manwha to check out if you're completely new to the medium, or if you just need some new recommendations. Which ones do you think everyone should read?

  • 1

    Solo Leveling

    Authors: Chugong (Story), Dubu (Art)

    Genres: Action, Fantasy

    Premise: In a world where hunters enter dungeons to destroy monsters for financial gain, Sung Jinwoo is notorious for being one of the weakest of them all. A hunter's abilities are typically set from the beginning, but a mysterious computer program offers him the chance to be the only person who can actually level up. This improves his chances in the dungeon, but it also makes his life a lot more complicated.

    Why Its Worth Reading: If you're an anime fan, there's a high chance that you've come across this one, since it's an extremely popular anime title. If you're a fan of the anime, the manhwa is worth a shot. But even if you've never seen the anime, it's still worthwhile: the protagonist's growing understanding of his powers and their implications is nuanced and deep, and the fights are intense and dark.

  • 2

    Omniscient Reader's Viewpoint

    Authors: singNsong and UMI (Story), Sleepy-C (Art)

    Genres: Action, Adventure, Fantasy

    Premise: After reading a novel called Three Ways to Survive in a Ruined World, office worker Kim Dokja is suddenly transported into the world of the book. He knows how the world is going to end, but he isn't willing to simply accept it. Instead, he decides to change things by undergoing scenarios - challenges operated by dokkaebi, or goblins from Korean mythology.  

    Why Its Worth Reading: In some ways, this reads like a typical isekai, but there are several plot twists that make it stand out. Most characters who know about some form of doom because they've entered a fictional realm are simply trying to save themselves - this takes things to a whole new level. Also, despite being pretty dark at the start, it can be surprisingly funny in spite of (or sometimes because of) its content.

  • 3

    The Greatest Estate Developer

    Authors: Lee Hyunmin (Story), Kim Hyunsoo (Art)

    Genres: Isekai, Fantasy, Comedy

    Premise: Civil engineering student Suho Kim falls asleep while reading a fantasy novel and wakes up in the world of his book. Now inhabiting the body of Lloyd Frontera, a booze-loving noble, he discovers that his new family is deep in debt. He decides to use his engineering knowledge - and a little bit of magic - to help his family overcome the mountain of financial problems they face.

    Why Its Worth Reading: It's hard to make an immoral character likable, yet this series makes it look easy. Although Lloyd is determined to swindle every last cent out of everyone he meets, he's so hilarious while doing it that you can't help but root for him to succeed. Also, the characters have fun, exaggerated facial expressions that make their designs all the more enjoyable.

  • 4

    The Legend of the Northern Blade

    Authors: Woogack (Story), Hae Min (Art)

    Genres: Martial Arts, Adventure, Fantasy

    Premise: After the leader of the Northern Heavenly Sect is accused of colluding with the enemy, he takes his own life. The martial arts group nearly disbands, but Jin Moowon is determined to keep the sect going. A group called the Silent Night is still a threat, and he isn't going to let them have their way, even if it means the whole world is against him.

    Why Its Worth Reading: This series features some incredible fight scenes that effectively showcase the protagonist's strengths. He fulfills many readers' desire to see a protagonist destroy their opponents with incredible powers, and he also has emotional development that lines up well with the plot. Overall, it's a well-crafted, exciting story, and it's hard to ask for more than that.

  • 5

    The Boxer

    Author: JH

    Genres: Sports, Drama

    Premise: Legendary boxing coach K hopes to find someone to pass on his skills to. When he comes across a young man who is standing there allowing himself to be beaten up without defending himself, he sees more than just a helpless victim - he sees somebody with the physical grace and strength needed to become a great boxer. For his part, the young man has only one question about the prospect of training: he wants to know whether hitting people is fun.

    Why Its Worth Reading: The reasons behind the young man's odd behavior are surprisingly complex. He's developed an almost supernatural ability to see time in slow motion, allowing him to react to things perfectly. Why can he do this? Because of intense childhood trauma. Though he doesn't seem to feel anything, his participation in boxing brings him to life. This is a great series from a character development perspective. Also, some of the boxing matches are just plain cool.

  • 6

    Sweet Home

    Authors: Carnby Kim (Story), Youngchan Hwang (Art)

    Genres: Horror, Thriller

    Premise: A suicidal high school student must work with the other people in his apartment building in order to survive in a post-apocalyptic world, where people transform into monsters whose forms reflect their most secret desires.

    Why Its Worth Reading: Apocalyptic monster-fighting aren't uncommon, but Sweet Home's spin on the subject is an imaginative one. The idea that the monsters reflect people's innermost secrets is a great opportunity for character development, and this series does an excellent job using that premise to the fullest. Also, just like Bastard, another project by these creators, Sweet Home has a strong sense of suspense.
